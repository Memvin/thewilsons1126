A few favourites if you’re visiting Perth

If you’ve got time around the wedding, here are some easy ideas — from Swan Valley wineries to beach sunsets. Everything is optional — pick what suits your vibe.

Swan Valley (near the venue)

Perfect for a relaxed day: wineries, breweries, distilleries, and lunch spots.

Easy plan: late breakfast → tasting or two → long lunch → one sweet stop → home.

Top wineries & breweries:
• Sandalford Wines — classic Swan Valley winery, great tastings
• Mandoon Estate — wine, beer & spirits all in one spot
• Lancaster Wines — smaller, relaxed, very Swan Valley
• Nikola Estate — historic winery with a restaurant onsite
• Feral Brewing Co. — casual brewery, easy food option
• Duckstein Brewery — German-style beer hall, fun atmosphere

Best for lunch:
• Homestead Brewery (Mandoon Estate) — relaxed & reliable
• Wild Swan at Mandoon — nicer sit-down lunch
• Sandalford Restaurant — if you want something more refined
• Nikola Estate Restaurant — good food, quieter setting

Tip: book lunch ahead on weekends — Swan Valley fills up quickly.

Snorkelling & beach spots

Perth has surprisingly good snorkelling — especially on calm days.

• Mettams Pool — sheltered reef, great for beginners
• Watermans Bay — clear water, reef close to shore
• Omeo Wreck (near Coogee) — snorkel around a shipwreck
• Cottesloe Beach — swimming & sunsets (less snorkelling)

Tip: mornings are best before the sea breeze picks up.

For food & drinks

A few easy areas to explore without overthinking it.

• Northbridge — lots of options, casual, walkable
• Leederville — cafés, bars, relaxed dinner spots
• Mount Lawley — little bars, wine, desserts
Search these areas on Google Maps and you’ll find something good quickly.

Margaret River & Busselton

A stunning region — but best as an overnight trip (3–4 hour drive from Perth).

Highlights:
• Busselton Jetty — longest timber jetty in the southern hemisphere
• World-class wineries & restaurants
• Beaches, caves, and coastal walks

If you’re short on time, Swan Valley is the easier option.

Quick day-trip ideas

If you’ve got a spare day and a car.

• Perth Hills — waterfalls/short walks when it’s cooler
• Hilarys Boat Harbour — easy coastal stroll + lunch
• Margaret River — amazing, but it’s far (better as an overnight trip)

A simple 2–3 day Perth itinerary

If you’re visiting from interstate or overseas, this is an easy, no-stress plan.

Day 1 — City & river
• Morning walk through Kings Park (best views in Perth)
• Coffee or lunch in the CBD or Elizabeth Quay
• Sunset drinks along the river or city bars

Day 2 — Coast or Fremantle
• Morning at the beach (Cottesloe, City Beach or Scarborough)
• Lunch in Fremantle or along the coast
• Afternoon wander, markets or pubs

Day 3 (optional) — Day trip
• Swan Valley wineries & lunch
• or Rottnest Island if the weather is good

Useful notes

• Perth can be hot in November — sunscreen + water are your best friends.
• If you’re planning drinks, rideshare is easiest (taxis can be more reliable later at night).
• If you’re staying in the Swan Valley, booking earlier is safer as options can fill up.
